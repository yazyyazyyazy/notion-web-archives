About us:
Nydia Rocha develops ongoing research into the minimal thickness of high-temperature clays, with the aim of integrating ceramics into furniture and lighting design. She began her artistic journey in painting — oil, acrylic, and encaustic — training at the Núcleo de Arte Contemporânea, where she was mentored by Antônio Peticov and Osmar Pinheiro, before finding ceramics as her primary form of expression.
Between 2016 and 2018, she attended the Célia Martins ceramics studio, participated in the Project Guidance Workshop with designer Gerson de Oliveira, and joined Coletivo 462 in the 2018 and 2019 editions of MADE - Mercado, Arte e Design. In 2024 and 2025, she presented lighting pieces in collaboration with Studio De La Cruz at SP-Arte. She holds a degree in Business Administration from the Centro Universitário Metodista Izabela Hendrix (MG), is originally from São José do Goiabal, and has lived in São Paulo for over two decades.
